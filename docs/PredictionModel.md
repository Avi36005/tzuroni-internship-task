# Probabilistic Prediction Pipeline

The Weather Prediction AI Trading Agent employs a multi-tiered quantitative modeling pipeline to forecast meteorological outcomes and calculate edges against market odds.

---

## 📐 Mathematical Formulation

The core forecasting engine combines historical climatological data, official forecasts, and unstructured news sentiment into a single calibrated probability distribution.

### 1. Climatological Prior $P(\text{Rain})$
The prior probability of rain (or high temperature) for a target calendar day is derived from a 5-year historical daily archive of observations (via Open-Meteo Archive API):
$$P(\text{Rain}) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbb{I}(\text{Precipitation}_i > 0.1\text{mm})}{N}$$
where $N$ is the total number of recorded historical days for the surrounding dates, and $\mathbb{I}$ is the indicator function.

### 2. Meteorological Forecast Likelihood $P(\text{Forecast})$
The forecast likelihood represents the current meteorological prediction generated by numerical weather prediction models (e.g. GFS, ECMWF) collected from Open-Meteo and NOAA.

### 3. News Sentiment Alignment $S$
A sentiment score $S \in [-1.0, 1.0]$ is extracted from news, alerts, and social discussions using the Research Agent. This shifts the raw probability:
- $S > 0$: Increases rain/anomaly probability (storm chatter, flood warnings).
- $S < 0$: Decreases rain probability (drought, clear sky chatter).
$$\text{Sentiment Shift} = S \times 10\%$$

### 4. Calibrated Ensemble Model
The raw probability is a weighted ensemble of the components:
$$\text{Raw Probability} = w_f \cdot P(\text{Forecast}) + w_c \cdot P(\text{Climatology}) + w_s \cdot \text{Sentiment Adjusted Forecast}$$
Default weights: $w_f = 65\%$, $w_c = 25\%$, $w_s = 10\%$.

To correct for human or model over-confidence at the tails, we apply a **logistic probability calibration (Platt scaling)** to map the raw probability to a calibrated probability $p$:
$$p = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-3.0 \cdot (\text{Raw Probability} - 0.5)}}$$
This calibrated probability $p$ is used for all downstream trading decisions.

---

## 📈 Market Edge & Expected Value (EV)

To trade, the Prediction Agent calculates the mathematical edge relative to the market implied probability ( YES share price $Price$):
$$\text{Edge} = p - Price$$

The Expected Value (EV) of buying 1 share at $Price$ (paying out $1.00 on win, $0.00 on loss) is:
$$\text{EV} = p \cdot (1.00) - Price$$

A trade is only authorized if:
1. **Edge is positive** ($\text{Edge} > 2\%$).
2. **Expected Value is positive** ($\text{EV} > \$0.00$).
Otherwise, the agent returns `NO TRADE`.
